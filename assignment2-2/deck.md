----

<div style="text-align: left;">

~~Group voting selects top 4 coupling concepts.~~ 

Each student leads ONE prototype. Prototypes must be:

- Physical  
- Testable  
- Low-fidelity  
- Based on simulated inputs  

</div>

----

NOT FULL products. Coupling mechanisms only. This is to Test, Validate, Refine the concepts.

---

The purpose is to make the embodied coupling testable. 

Your prototypes must:

- Be physical, and materialize the selected coupling mechanism

----

- Be testable, 
    - Be functional enough to test the interaction and its modulation effect
    - Produce an observable bodily experience tied to the infrastructural variable and planetary consequence

----

- Be simple, low-fidelity, and material-focused

----

- Use manual or simulated inputs (no live AI integration required)

---

Your prototypes should NOT:

- Be full products or finished interfaces
- Rely on complex electronics unless simplified
- Attempt interface/system integration (that happens in Assignment 3)

---

Examples of acceptable prototypes:

- A heating element that increases in intensity according to the simulated infrastructure state
- A mechanical resistance module (spring/lever) that tightens with escalation
- A vibrating feedback device that increases rhythm with simulated load
