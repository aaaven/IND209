#### Four Options for the Design

Choose **one** direction as the main form of your AI interface. Each direction defines a different **relationship between the body and the device**, and the deployment context.  


</div>

---

<div style="text-align: left;">

#### 1. Wearable AI Interface

Wearable devices are directly attached to, worn on, or carried by the body.Examples:

- Ring, watch, necklace, bracelet, or other jewelry-like devices  
- Body-attached systems  

**Scale:** Intimate  

**Context:** Individual / Private

</div>

---

<div style="text-align: left;">

#### 2. Portable AI Interface

Portable interfaces are hand-held or easy to carry across different everyday situations. Examples:

- Phone-like AI interaction device  
- Hand-held AI console  
- Add-on module for existing devices, such as a phone, tablet, or laptop  

**Scale:** Intimate  

**Context:** Individual / Private

</div>

---

<div style="text-align: left;">

#### 3. Desktop AI Interaction Device

Desktop devices stay within an immediate working, studying, or living environment. Examples:

- Standalone desktop AI interface  
- Table-top machine or AI console  
- Speculative furniture integrated with AI interaction  

**Scale:** Immediate / Ambient  

**Context:** Individual / Semi-private

</div>

---

<div style="text-align: left;">

#### 4. Public AI Interaction System

Public AI systems are designed for shared, institutional, or collective contexts. Examples:

- Shared AI terminal  
- Kiosk  
- Public AI booth  
- Installation-like AI interaction system  

**Scale:**  Ambient 

**Context:** Shared by multiple users / Public

</div>
